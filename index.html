Problem Around Perception

Sanjesh

My wife and I love our home we live in Waterview. Our community is so good, but we are worried. Our local school is only a decile 3. Our daughter will be starting school next year, We have applied out of zone on the ballet for Grey Lynn Primary as we have heard its a good area and it's a Decile 9. We hope we get in, but we are preparing ourselves to have to get another mortgage in case we need to move. It may mean having to get our parents to come live together with us as house prices are much more expensive but education is important to us.

Problem Around Perception

THE PROBLEM IS NOT THE FUNDING SYSTEM,
BUT THE PERCEPTION.
Have you ever been to a decile one library? Or a decile three post office? Or perhaps you've been to a decile eight doctor? We don't label any other service in our society the way that we label our schools.

I can't think of any reason to want to do that to a community in an already obviously struggling area trying to pull itself up day in day out. Stamping yet another badge, projecting more clearly than anything else that on a scale of one to ten, bottom to top: you are not as good as the rest. In fact, only one tenth as good as the best. You're parents are one tenth as successful. Are ten times less educated. They do a job that requires 10% of the skills. They're 10% as flash. How is this type of framing positive for a child or a community?
Derek Handley

Recommendations

High decile is not a guarantee that it will be right fit of your child. We need to reframe decile to ensure that the we see the child's needs first. An increased accountability around resource and funding, higher engagement and connection with communities, build resilience and belonging, We need to identify the risk factors,  work together and in-partnership with supportive networks, and wraparound school initiatives to ensure positive and systemic change.

The Decile 9 project is dispelling the perception surrounding decile ratings and lower deciles having undesirable educational outcomes. The perception that higher decile schools offer better education is largely a myth. We are recommending an alternative tool to explore the data that can help people make informed decisions for the local area.
